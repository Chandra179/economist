# Macroeconomics

### Basic Economic Concepts

* Production Possibilities Curve (PPC): Models scarcity and trade-offs.
* Equilibrium: Where supply meets demand.
* Absolute and Comparative Advantage: Determining who should produce what (requires calculation of opportunity costs).
* Economic Systems: \* Free Market System: Decisions by individuals.
  * Capitalism: Private ownership of resources.
  * Mixed Economy: Blend of market and government intervention.
* Circular Flow Model: Shows the flow of goods/services and money between households and firms.

***

### Measuring Economic Performance (GDP)

* Macro Measures: \* GDP: Total value of all final goods produced in a country.
  * GDP per capita: GDP divided by population (standard of living).
* Excluded from GDP:
  * Intermediate Goods: Not included to avoid double counting (e.g., price of a finished car includes the tires).
  * Non-production Transactions: Financial assets (stocks, bonds) and used goods (old cars, used clothes).
  * Non-market Activities: Illegal activities (black market) and unpaid work (household chores).
* Calculating GDP:
  * Expenditure Approach: $$C + I + G + (X - M)$$
  * Income Approach: Sum of all factor payments.
* Nominal vs. Real:
  * Nominal GDP: Measured in current prices; does not account for inflation.
  * Real GDP: Adjusts for inflation (the best measure of economic growth).
* The Business Cycle:
  * Recession: A 6-month period of decline in Real GDP.

***

### Unemployment & Inflation

* Labor Force: People 16+ who are working or actively looking for work.
  * Labor Force Participation Rate: Percentage of the population in the labor force.
* Unemployment Rate:
  * Frictional: People between jobs or seasonal work.
  * Structural: Skills are obsolete (Technological unemployment).
  * Cyclical: Caused by a recession.
  * Natural Rate of Unemployment: The sum of frictional and structural unemployment.
* Inflation: General rise in prices.
  * Consumer Price Index (CPI): Measures the cost of a "market basket" of goods.
  * GDP Deflator: A broader measure of price changes in the whole economy.
  * Nominal & Real Wage/Interest Rates: Nominal is the dollar amount; Real is adjusted for purchasing power.
* Causes of Inflation:
  * Quantity Theory of Money: Printing too much money ($$M \times V = P \times Y$$).
  * Demand-Pull Inflation: "Too much money chasing too few goods."
  * Cost-Push Inflation: Higher production costs drive up prices (Stagflation).

***

### Aggregate Demand/Supply & Fiscal Policy

* Aggregate Demand (AD): Total spending in the economy.
  * Shifters/Effects: Wealth effect, Interest rate effect, and Foreign trade effect.
* Aggregate Supply (AS): Short Run (SRAS) and Long Run (LRAS).
* Economic Gaps: Recessionary Gap and Inflationary Gap.
* Fiscal Policy: Government taxing and spending.
  * Expansionary: Lower taxes, higher spending (to fix a recession).
  * Contractionary: Higher taxes, lower spending (to fix inflation).
  * Budget Deficit: Spending more than earned in a year.
  * National Debt: Accumulation of all past deficits.
* The Multiplier Effect:
  * MPC (Marginal Propensity to Consume) and MPS (Marginal Propensity to Save).
  * Spending Multiplier Calculation: $$1 / MPS$$.

***

### Money, Banking, & Monetary Policy

* Money: Barter system, Commodity money (gold), and Fiat money (paper).
  * Liquidity: The ease with which an asset can be used as a medium of exchange.
  * M1 Money Supply: Highest liquidity (Currency in circulation, checkable deposits, traveler's checks).
* Banking:
  * Federal Reserve (The Fed): Central bank of the US.
  * Fractional Reserve Banking: Banks hold only a portion of deposits in reserve.
  * Bank Balance Sheet: Assets vs. Liabilities.
  * Money Multiplier: $$1 / Reserve\ Requirement$$.
* The Money Market: \* Transaction Demand: Money for daily purchases.
  * Asset Demand: Money held as a store of value.
* The 3 Shifters of Money Supply (Fed Tools):
  1. Reserve Requirement: The % banks must keep.
  2. The Discount Rate: Interest the Fed charges banks.
  3. Open Market Operations: Buying or selling government bonds.
* Federal Funds Rate: Interest rate banks charge each other for overnight loans.
* Loanable Funds Market: Where borrowers and savers interact to determine the real interest rate.

***

### International Trade & Foreign Exchange

* Balance of Payments (BOP): Record of all transactions between countries.
  * Current Account: Trades in goods/services, investment income, and net transfers.
  * Financial/Capital Account: Purchase and sale of assets (Net capital outflow and inflow).
* Trade Balance: Trade Surplus vs. Trade Deficit.
* Foreign Exchange (Forex):
  * Appreciation: Currency value goes up.
  * Depreciation: Currency value goes down.
  * Forex Shifters: Changes in Tastes, Relative Incomes, Inflation, and Interest Rates.
